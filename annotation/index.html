Capability Definitions

  • Retrieval: Context: Forecasts would rely on a human's ability to retrieve relevant information from the context and distinguish it from irrelevant information.
  • Retrieval: Memory: Forecasts would rely on facts from memory or a knowledge base, such as relevant physical constants or quantitative laws.
  • Reasoning: Deduction:Forecasts would rely on a human's ability to draw valid inferences from the context, e.g. inferring from the context that certain values are logically impossible to occur.
  • Reasoning: Causal: Forecasts would rely on a human's ability to reason about cause and effect from the context, such as reasoning about the impacts of various actions described in the context.
  • Reasoning: Math:Forecasts would rely on a human's ability to perform mathematics over the context, such as arithmetic or solving equations.
  • Reasoning: Analogy:Forecasts would rely on a human's ability to forecast by way of analogy between entities or events, for instance, applying knowledge from a past event that is similar to an upcoming one.
